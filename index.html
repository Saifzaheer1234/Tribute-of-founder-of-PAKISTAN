QUAID-E-AZAM

1876-1948

“Expect the best, prepare for the worst.”

BIOGRAPHY

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was the founder of Pakistan and a lawyer and politician who fought for the rights of Muslims in British India. He was born on 25 December 1876 in Karachi to an Ismaili Shia Muslim family. He received his early education in Karachi and then studied law in the U.K. He returned to India in 1896 and married a Parsi woman Ruttie Petit. He led the All-India Muslim League from 1913 and became the father of the Pakistani nation. He died on 11 September 19481.
